# Camber Co LTD - Competitive Edge

_Every AI consultancy says "we help businesses use AI." Here's how Camber Co actually stands out._

---

## You Build, Not Just Advise

- Most AI consultants are ex-management consultants who make slides. Camber Co actually ships working products.
- Charlie has built full apps end-to-end with AI agents.
- Your pitch: "I don't just tell you what to do. I build it, deploy it, and train your team to use it."
- This is your single biggest differentiator. Protect it. Never become a slide deck shop.

## You Eat Your Own Cooking

- You use AI tools daily in your own work. You know what actually works and what's hype.
- You can demo real workflows, not theoretical ones.
- Credibility: "I do 120 hours of work in 20 hours using the tools I'm recommending to you."

## The "Before and After" Demo

- Record screen captures of a manual process, then show the automated version
- Nothing sells like a live demo. Build 3-4 industry-specific demos you can show prospects.
- Example: "Here's a tradesman manually scheduling jobs via text messages. Here's the same thing handled by a bot in 30 seconds."

## Niche Opportunities Worth Exploring

### Recruitment Agencies

- CV screening, candidate matching, interview scheduling, client communication
- Recruitment is drowning in repetitive admin. Huge willingness to pay for automation.

### Estate Agents

- Property descriptions generated from photos, viewing scheduling, buyer/seller matching
- Follow-up sequences automated, market report generation
- Very relationship-driven industry, so position as "we free up your time for the human stuff"

### Accountancy Firms

- Document extraction from receipts and invoices
- Client onboarding automation
- Tax deadline reminders and document chasing
- Many small firms still run on manual processes

### Construction and Trades

- The tradesperson offering could scale into a productised SaaS
- If the bot works for one plumber, it works for 10,000 plumbers
- Consider: build it as a service first, then productise it once you've validated demand

### Legal Firms (Small/Mid)

- Contract review assistance, legal research summarisation
- Client intake automation, document assembly
- Be careful with positioning here. "AI-assisted" not "AI replaces your solicitor"

### NHS and Healthcare Adjacent

- Patient communication, appointment scheduling, follow-up
- Compliance-heavy but massive demand for efficiency
- Requires specific data handling (GDPR, NHS DSPT) which is a barrier to entry that protects you

## Productisation Opportunities

- Any service you deliver more than 5 times can probably be turned into a product
- The tradesperson bot could become a subscription SaaS
- AI training workshops could become an online course
- The AI readiness audit could become a self-serve tool with a paid "full audit" upsell
- Think: "do the service manually first, learn what works, then automate yourself"

## Partnership and Channel Opportunities

- **Accountants** refer their clients to you (they talk to every small business)
- **Web agencies** partner with you to add AI services to their offering
- **Business coaches and mentors** recommend you to their clients
- **Coworking spaces** host your workshops, you get access to their members
- **Trade associations** (Federation of Master Builders, etc) offer you as a member benefit
- These channels are worth more than any marketing budget

## Things To Be Honest About

- You're new. Own it. "Camber Co is a new consultancy but I've been building with AI for years" is honest and compelling.
- Not every business needs AI. Be willing to tell a prospect "you don't need me" and they'll trust you more.
- AI is moving fast. Commit to continuous learning. What works today might be outdated in 6 months.
- Some clients will want things you can't deliver. Know your limits and subcontract or refer out.
- As a solo founder, capacity is your constraint. Be selective about which engagements you take.
