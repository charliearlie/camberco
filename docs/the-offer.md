# Camber Co LTD - The Offer

_"AI consulting" is not an offer. A specific problem you solve for a specific person is an offer._

---

## Target Customer

- Who exactly are you selling to? (SMEs? Mid-market? Enterprise?)
- What industry or industries? (Pick 1-2 to start, expand later)
- Who is the buyer? (Owner? Ops Manager? CTO? Finance Director?)
- What size company? (5 people? 50? 500?)

## The Problem You Solve

- Write it in one sentence a non-technical person would understand
- Examples to consider:
  - "We reduce your admin overhead by 40% using AI automation"
  - "We help dev teams ship 3-5x faster with AI tooling"
  - "We replace manual business processes with AI workflows that run 24/7"
- Test: would a business owner hear this and say "I need that"?

## What Does an Engagement Look Like?

- **Option A: Audit then implement** - Paid discovery (1-2 days), recommendations report, then implementation project
- **Option B: Retainer** - Monthly fee, ongoing AI optimisation and support
- **Option C: Project-based** - Scoped deliverable, fixed price, defined timeline
- Which model fits your skills and your target customer best?

## Pricing

- Day rates: what's realistic for your market? (£500-1000/day for UK SME consulting)
- Project fees: how do you scope and price a project?
- Retainers: what's the minimum monthly fee that makes it worth your time?
- Research what competitors charge. Don't undercharge to win clients.

## Proof and Credibility

- What have you built or delivered that demonstrates you can do this?
- List every relevant project, result, or metric you can reference
- Package 2-3 of these as simple case studies (problem, what you did, result)
- You don't need a portfolio site. A well-written Google Doc or PDF works.
- Strong candidates: the 120hrs-in-20hrs productivity story, WhatsApp Business automation, Football IQ as a solo AI-built product
