# Camber Co LTD - Pricing Structure

_Price on value delivered, not hours spent. If you save a business £50k/year, charging £5k is a bargain for them._

---

## Tier 1: Entry Point (Get in the Door)

### AI Readiness Audit

- Half-day on-site or remote assessment
- Deliverable: written report with prioritised recommendations
- Price: £750 - £1,500
- Purpose: this is your lead magnet. Every audit should convert to a bigger engagement.

### AI Workshop (Team Training)

- Half-day: £1,500 - £2,000
- Full-day: £2,500 - £3,500
- Per group of up to 15 people
- Tailored to their industry and roles

## Tier 2: Project Work (One-Off Delivery)

### Workflow Automation Build

- Scoped project: automate a specific business process
- Price: £2,000 - £10,000 depending on complexity
- Examples: automated invoicing, lead qualification, customer onboarding, report generation

### AI Chatbot / Customer Service Bot

- WhatsApp, website, or Messenger bot
- Price: £3,000 - £8,000 build + £200-500/month maintenance
- Include: setup, training on their data, testing, handoff rules

### Internal Knowledge Assistant

- AI trained on their docs, processes, SOPs
- Price: £5,000 - £15,000 depending on data volume
- Ongoing: £500-1,000/month for maintenance and updates

### Sole Trader / Tradesperson Package

- All-in-one: scheduling, invoicing, customer comms bot
- Price: £1,500 - £3,000 setup + £100-200/month
- Lower price point but high volume. Could become productised.

## Tier 3: Ongoing Retainers (Recurring Revenue)

### AI Support Retainer

- Monthly support, optimisation, new automation builds
- Price: £1,500 - £3,000/month (small business)
- Price: £3,000 - £7,000/month (mid-market)
- Minimum 3-month commitment

### Fractional CTO

- Charlie embedded 1-2 days/week in the client's business
- Price: £3,000 - £6,000/month
- Strategic AI roadmap, vendor management, team training
- Minimum 6-month commitment recommended
- Capacity: 1-2 fractional CTO clients max at any time

## Pricing Principles

- Never charge by the hour. Hourly rates punish you for being fast.
- Always price based on value to the client, not cost to you
- If an automation saves them £3k/month in staff time, charging £5k to build it is easy to justify
- Offer payment plans for larger projects (50% upfront, 50% on delivery)
- Early clients get a discount in exchange for a case study and testimonial
- Revisit pricing every 3 months as you build credibility

## Capacity Planning (Solo Founder Reality)

- You can't deliver everything at once. Prioritise high-value engagements.
- Fractional CTO + 1-2 projects is probably your max capacity
- When demand exceeds capacity, that's when you hire specialist subcontractors for delivery
- Joe handles marketing so you can focus entirely on sales conversations and delivery
