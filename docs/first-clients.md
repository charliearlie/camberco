# Camber Co LTD - First Clients

_Your first 3 clients will come from people you already know. Not from marketing._

---

## Warm Network Audit

- List every business owner, MD, ops manager, or decision-maker you know personally
- Include: former employers, clients from previous work, friends who run businesses, LinkedIn connections
- Be honest about the strength of each relationship (close friend vs acquaintance)
- Ask Joe to do the same from his network since he'll be involved in outreach

## Who Has a Problem Right Now?

- Go through your list and identify who is:
  - Drowning in manual admin work
  - Complaining about software costs
  - Struggling with customer communication
  - Running a growing team with messy processes
  - Curious about AI but doesn't know where to start

## The Approach

- Don't pitch. Have a conversation. "I'm starting something new helping businesses use AI to save time and money. Can I buy you a coffee and pick your brain about what's eating your time?"
- Offer a free 1-hour audit for your first 2-3 clients to build case studies
- After the audit, present findings and a paid proposal
- The free work is an investment in proof, not a habit

## Joe's Role in Client Acquisition

- Joe handles marketing: LinkedIn content, outreach messaging, lead generation
- Charlie handles sales conversations and delivery
- Clear split: Joe fills the pipeline, Charlie closes and delivers

## Referral Engine

- After every engagement, ask: "Who else do you know who might benefit from this?"
- A happy client referring you is 10x more effective than any marketing
- Consider a referral incentive (discount on next engagement, or a flat fee)

## What to Skip For Now

- Paid advertising
- Content marketing strategy beyond what Joe is doing
- Social media calendars
- SEO
- All of this matters later. None of it matters before your first paying client.
